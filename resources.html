Evidence-Based Trauma Resources

These resources reflect the scientific, psychological, and behavioral foundations behind the work on this site. They offer evidence-based insight into trauma, child development, nervous-system adaptation, identity harm, spiritual abuse, addiction, and recovery.

Government & National Agencies

  • SAMHSA – Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration
    Gold-standard trauma information, ACEs, behavioral health, recovery resources.
  • CDC – Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACE) Study
    The foundational research on developmental trauma and long-term health.
  • National Child Traumatic Stress Network (NCTSN)
    Developmental trauma, dissociation, and childhood adversity.
  • National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH)
    Neuroscience, stress physiology, and trauma research.

Trauma, Attachment & Developmental Science

  • Dr. Bessel van der Kolk – The Body Keeps the Score (Resources)
    One of the most respected voices on trauma and the body.
  • Dr. Stephen Porges – Polyvagal Theory
    Nervous system responses, safety vs. threat states.
  • Center on the Developing Child – Harvard University
    Brain development under stress, toxic stress, resilience.
  • Child Mind Institute
    Trauma impact on children and emotional development.

Identity, LGBTQ+ Survivorship, and Religious Trauma

  • The Trevor Project
    LGBTQ+ youth and trauma-informed crisis support.
  • Gay & Lesbian Medical Association (GLMA)
    Health data related to LGBTQ+ trauma and discrimination.
  • Reclamation Collective – Religious Trauma Resources
    Support for religious trauma and spiritual abuse recovery.
  • International Cultic Studies Association (ICSA)
    Research on coercive control, spiritual abuse, and identity harm.

Addiction, Opioid Childhoods & Family Systems

  • National Association for Children of Addiction (NACoA)
    ACE-related research and support for children of addicted parents.
  • NIH – National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA)
    Data on addiction, neurobiology, and intergenerational impact.
  • Adult Children of Dysfunctional Families (ACA)
    Emotional survival patterns from chaotic or addicted homes.
